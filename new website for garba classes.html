About Garba

Garba is a traditional folk dance from Gujarat, India, performed to celebrate culture, rhythm, and community spirit with colorful clothes and joyful music. The dance form originates from the devotional Garba dances that were performed in honor of Goddess Durga.

At Utsav Garba Classes, we preserve the authentic form of this beautiful dance while also incorporating modern elements to make it accessible to everyone. Our classes cater to beginners who want to learn the basics as well as advanced dancers looking to refine their skills.

Garba is more than just a dance - it's a celebration of life, community, and tradition. Through our classes, we aim to keep this rich cultural tradition alive while making it enjoyable for everyone.

Meet Your Instructor

Dr. Montu Chadotara

With over 25 years of experience in performing and teaching Garba, Dr. Chadotara is a renowned expert in Gujarati folk dance. He holds a PhD in Performing Arts from Gujarat University and has trained under some of the most respected Garba masters in India.

Dr. Chadotara has performed at major cultural festivals across India and has taught thousands of students worldwide. His teaching methodology emphasizes not just the steps but also the cultural significance and spiritual aspects of Garba.

"Garba is not just a dance—it's a celebration of life, culture, and community," says Dr. Chadotara. "My goal is to share this beautiful art form while preserving its traditional roots."
